FAQ

Why do I start with a paid diagnostic session?

Because it’s the fastest way to get real answers. It's so I don't waste your time with sales pitches, and don't waste your money with over-engineered solutions. Look, I get it - paying a little upfront deters some clients. The most important piece of this work is understanding your problem and proposing solutions that fit your needs and budget. Sometimes the best solution is so easy I can fix things and keep your bill at 15 minutes of work. Sometimes the best solution is so simple you don't need me at all. Sometimes it's so complicated you need a big agency instead. No one's going to tell you any of those things for free after investing an hour of their time trying to sell you.

What if you can fix my issue during the session?

If it’s a small fix, we’ll handle it in the session. If it’s larger, you’ll leave with a diagnostic report and technical blueprint—a standalone deliverable that identifies the root cause, outlines the necessary steps to fix it, and provides a realistic estimate.

Do you offer packages or fixed pricing?

No. Sometimes it makes sense to customize an ongoing maintenance package for clients that really need that, but otherwise everything is straightforward hourly pricing, even major projects like full site builds from scratch.

What do you need from me before we start diagnostic session?

To make the most of our time, I require a few minimum essentials to be submitted when you book:

  • Site URL: The address of the WordPress site needing attention.
  • The "What & Why": A brief description of the specific issue and your end goal for the session.

The more, the better: While those are the basics, feel free to share error logs, recent plugin changes, and anything else you think helps me better understand the issue. The more context I have beforehand, the less time I spend investigating the "knowns," and the more time we can dedicate to active diagnostics and potential fixes during your session.
